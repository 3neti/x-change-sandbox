# Success Stage + Countdown UX Decision

## Background

The claim success experience can be composed from two independent sources:

1. **Success rider stages** (visual success experience rendered by x-rider)
2. **Redirect countdown** (compiled claim experience generated by x-change)

Both may be present at the same time.

Example:

```text
Claim Successful
↓
Display success stages
↓
Display redirect countdown
↓
Navigate through redirect gate
↓
External rider destination
```

The question was whether the presence of success stages should suppress the redirect countdown.

---

## Decision

### Render Both

When a successful claim contains:

- one or more success rider stages
- an enabled redirect countdown

the success page SHALL render both.

```text
Success rider stages
+
Redirect countdown
```

The redirect countdown is not suppressed by the existence of success stages.

---

## Rationale

The two elements communicate different concerns.

### Success Rider Stages

Success stages communicate:

```text
What happened?
```

Examples:

- Funds disbursed
- Claim completed
- Account credited
- Voucher redeemed

These are outcome-oriented messages.

---

### Redirect Countdown

The countdown communicates:

```text
What happens next?
```

Examples:

- Returning to merchant
- Opening follow-up experience
- Continuing onboarding
- Navigating to dashboard

These are navigation-oriented messages.

---

## Why Suppression Was Rejected

An alternative considered was:

```text
If success stages exist:
    Hide redirect countdown
```

Result:

```text
Success stages
→ sudden redirect
```

This was rejected because redirects can feel abrupt and surprising.

The claimant should have a clear indication that navigation is about to occur.

---

## Navigation Rule

The countdown must never navigate directly to a rider URL.

Instead:

```text
Success Page
    ↓
Redirect Countdown
    ↓
Claim Redirect Endpoint
    ↓
Redirect Gate
    ↓
Resolved Rider Destination
```

The countdown always targets:

```text
redirectEndpoint
```

Example:

```text
/x/claim/TEST123/redirect
```

and never:

```text
https://example.com/raw-rider-url
```

This preserves:

- centralized redirect policy
- auditing opportunities
- future authorization gates
- analytics hooks
- destination abstraction

---

## Current Expected UX

### Scenario A

Success stages only

```text
✓ Claim completed

Funds have been credited.
```

---

### Scenario B

Countdown only

```text
✓ Claim completed

Redirecting in 5 seconds...
```

---

### Scenario C

Success stages + countdown

```text
✓ Funds disbursed

Your account has been credited.

Redirecting in 5 seconds...
```

This is the preferred and supported behavior.

```text
renders success visual stages together with redirect countdown when both exist
does not let success visual stages suppress redirect countdown
passes redirectEndpoint to RiderCountdown
```

---

### x-rider

Covered by:

```text

```

Assertions include:

```text
countdown navigates to redirect endpoint
countdown never navigates directly to rider URL
```

---

## Architectural Outcome

The success page becomes a composition surface:

```text
Compiled Claim Experience
+
Rider Experience
```

where:

- x-change owns claim lifecycle behavior
- x-rider owns presentation behavior
- redirect policy remains centralized behind the redirect gate

This keeps the UX predictable while preserving future flexibility for authorization, analytics, auditing, and conditional navigation.
